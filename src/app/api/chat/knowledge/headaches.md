# Headaches

## Common Types

**Tension headache** — the most common type. Feels like a tight band around your head. Usually caused by stress, poor posture, or lack of sleep. Both sides of the head are typically affected.

**Migraine** — intense, throbbing pain usually on one side of the head. Often comes with nausea, sensitivity to light and sound, and can last hours to days. Some people see flashing lights or zigzag lines before a migraine starts (called an aura).

**Cluster headache** — severe pain around one eye or one side of the head. Comes in clusters over weeks or months, then disappears for a while. Less common but very painful.

**Sinus headache** — pressure and pain around your forehead, cheeks, and eyes. Usually comes with a blocked or runny nose. Often linked to a cold or sinus infection.

## Common Causes

Dehydration, stress, lack of sleep, too much screen time, skipping meals, caffeine withdrawal, alcohol, eye strain, or tension in your neck and shoulders.

## Self-Care

Drink plenty of water. Rest in a quiet, dark room. Apply a cold or warm compress to your forehead. Over-the-counter pain relief like paracetamol or ibuprofen can help. Try to maintain a regular sleep schedule.

## When to Seek Help

See a GP if headaches happen more than 15 days a month, keep getting worse, don't respond to over-the-counter pain relief, or started after a head injury. Call 999 if you have a sudden, severe headache unlike anything you've experienced before — especially with a stiff neck, confusion, vision problems, weakness, or slurred speech.
