# Sore Throat

## Common Causes

**Viral infections** — the most common cause. Includes the common cold, flu, and COVID-19. Usually clears up on its own within a week.

**Strep throat** — a bacterial infection that causes a very painful throat, often without a cough. You may see white patches on your tonsils and have swollen neck glands. Needs antibiotics from a GP.

**Tonsillitis** — swollen, red tonsils that may have white spots. Common in children and young adults. Can be caused by viruses or bacteria.

**Allergies** — postnasal drip from allergies can irritate your throat, causing a scratchy or sore feeling. Usually comes with sneezing, itchy eyes, and a runny nose.

**Dry air or irritants** — breathing dry indoor air (especially in winter with heating on), smoking, or exposure to pollution can dry out and irritate your throat.

## Home Remedies

Drink warm fluids like tea with honey (honey soothes the throat). Gargle with warm salt water (half a teaspoon of salt in a glass of warm water). Use throat lozenges or hard sweets to keep the throat moist. Stay hydrated. Use a humidifier if the air is dry. Rest your voice if it hurts to talk.

## When to Seek Help

See a GP if your sore throat lasts more than a week, you have difficulty swallowing or breathing, you have a persistent fever above 38.3°C, you see white patches on the back of your throat, or you have a rash. Seek urgent care if you have difficulty breathing or feel like your throat is closing up.
