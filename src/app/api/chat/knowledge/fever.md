# Fever

## What Is a Fever?

A fever is when your body temperature rises above the normal range. Normal body temperature is around 37°C (98.6°F), but it varies slightly from person to person and throughout the day. A fever is generally considered to be 38°C (100.4°F) or higher.

Fever is not an illness itself — it's your body's natural response to fighting an infection. It means your immune system is working.

## Temperature Ranges

**Low-grade fever (37.5–38°C)** — mild elevation. Common with minor viral infections. Usually not concerning on its own.

**Moderate fever (38–39°C)** — your body is actively fighting an infection. Common with colds, flu, and other infections. Rest and fluids are important.

**High fever (39–40°C)** — more significant. You'll likely feel quite unwell. Worth monitoring closely and considering seeing a GP, especially if it lasts more than 2-3 days.

**Very high fever (above 40°C)** — seek medical attention. This level of fever can be dangerous, especially in children and elderly people.

## Common Causes

Viral infections (cold, flu, COVID-19), bacterial infections (urinary tract infections, ear infections, strep throat), inflammatory conditions, and sometimes medications. In most cases, a fever is caused by a common infection and resolves on its own.

## Self-Care

Drink plenty of fluids — water, clear soups, and diluted juice. Rest as much as possible. Wear light clothing and keep the room comfortable. Paracetamol or ibuprofen can help bring down a fever and ease discomfort. Sponging with lukewarm (not cold) water can help. Do not bundle up in heavy blankets even if you feel cold.

## When to Seek Help

See a GP if a fever lasts more than 3 days, keeps coming back, or is accompanied by a rash, stiff neck, severe headache, persistent vomiting, or confusion. For babies under 3 months with any fever (38°C or above), seek medical help immediately. For children, seek help if they are unusually drowsy, have a rash that doesn't fade when pressed, or are not drinking fluids. Call 999 if there is a fever with a stiff neck, confusion, difficulty breathing, or a non-blanching rash — these could be signs of meningitis.
