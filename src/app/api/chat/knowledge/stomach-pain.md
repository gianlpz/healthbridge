# Stomach Pain

## Common Causes

**Indigestion** — a burning or uncomfortable feeling in your upper abdomen after eating. Often caused by eating too fast, fatty or spicy food, stress, or too much caffeine or alcohol.

**Gastritis** — inflammation of the stomach lining. Causes a gnawing or burning pain in the upper stomach, nausea, and feeling full quickly. Can be triggered by painkillers like ibuprofen, alcohol, or an infection called H. pylori.

**Bloating and gas** — trapped wind can cause sharp, crampy pains that move around your abdomen. Common after eating certain foods like beans, onions, broccoli, or fizzy drinks.

**Constipation** — not going to the toilet often enough or finding it hard to go. Causes cramping, bloating, and pain in the lower abdomen. Usually caused by not enough fibre, water, or physical activity.

**Food poisoning** — sudden stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhoea. Usually starts hours after eating contaminated food and clears up within a few days.

**Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)** — a long-term condition causing stomach cramps, bloating, diarrhoea, and constipation. Symptoms come and go and are often triggered by stress or certain foods.

## Dietary Advice

Eat smaller meals more frequently. Avoid trigger foods (spicy, fatty, or acidic foods). Drink plenty of water. Eat more fibre for constipation. Avoid eating late at night. Try to reduce stress, which can worsen stomach issues.

## Warning Signs

Seek urgent medical help if you have severe pain that's getting worse, blood in your vomit or stool, a hard and tender abdomen, pain with a high fever, or if the pain is in your lower right side (could be appendicitis). Sudden, severe stomach pain that started quickly should be assessed by a doctor.
